The core system

The Batch Creation System

Bailey's System
The 3-Block Batch Creation System Tap to read ▼

Block 1: Research. You sit down and figure out what's trending right now. What people are actually buying or watching. What's selling, not what looks pretty to you.

Block 2: Create. You take what you found, write a prompt, generate your images or videos or content, and edit it.

Block 3: Publish. Get it out into the world. Listings, mockups, scheduling, posting. Done.

Each block can be anywhere from 30 minutes to 2 hours depending on how much time you have. Full afternoon? Do all three. An hour before the kids wake up? Do one block. The system scales to your schedule.

Bailey's real Tuesday: Kids are at school. I sit down with coffee. 30 minutes of research, what sublimation designs are trending this week? I find three niches. Then an hour creating: ChatGPT prompts, generate in Nano, clean up in Canva. Then 30 minutes publishing: mockups done, listings written, everything scheduled. Two hours. Done for the day.
That's the system. Every path. Every week. It's not complicated. It's not glamorous. But it works.

The operating system

Learn. Build. Delegate. Stack.

Bailey's Operating System
Learn. Build. Delegate. Stack. Explained Tap to read ▼

Learn one path. Really learn it. Not watch videos about it. Actually DO it.

Build the system for it. Make it repeatable. Research day, create day, publish day. Same rhythm every week.

Delegate. Hand off the parts you don't need to do yourself. Bulk creation, scheduling, listing. "Imagine checking your Etsy app over morning coffee and seeing 12 sales from designs your VA listed last week using prompts you wrote in 20 minutes. That's what a system looks like."

Stack. Move to the next path. That's how Bailey went from one income stream to multiple.

Bailey's rule: Don't try to start your second path until the first one has a repeatable system.

Bailey on ADHD
Bailey's ADHD survival strategies Tap to read ▼

"I have ADHD. I know what it's like to have the whole plan and still not follow through. Not because you're lazy. Because your brain doesn't work that way."

"When I've had to just focus on one thing and not have the ability to jump to something else when I feel burnt out or not creatively inspired, that's when I just don't do anything at all. I shut down."

"But when I have multiple things to work on? That actually helps me focus on at least one thing. If I'm burned out from designs, I switch to making a video. The variety isn't a distraction. It's a survival strategy."

"One thing that has saved me in Phase 1 is having little checklists of steps for each path. Research block, check. Creation block, check. Publish block, check. My ADHD brain likes being able to actually check things off. And each day has something new and exciting."
